Multiple Object Tracking in Complex Environments Workshop

Three challenges in long video, occluded object and diverse motion

August 00th, 0:00 am PST, ECCV 2022

Overview

Abstract

Multiple object tracking aims to localize and associate objects of interest along time, and serves as fundamental technologies in many practical applications, such as visual surveillance, public security, video analysis, and human-computer interaction.

Computer vision systems nowadays have achieved great performance in simple scenes of multiple object tracking, such as MOT dataset and DAVIS dataset. However, they are not as robust as the human vision system, especially in complex environments.

To advance current vision systems performance in complex environments, our workshop explores three settings: (a) long video (b) occluded object (c) diverse motion.

Three challenges consists of:
  • 4th Large-scale Video Object Segmentation Challenge: Long Video Track
  • 2nd Occluded Video Instance Segmentation Challenge
  • 1st Multiple People Tracking in Group Dance Challenge
Distinctive Properties
  • Long videos. The average video duration and the average instance duration are 12.77s and 10.05s respectively.
  • Severe occlusions. The most distinctive property of our OVIS dataset is that a large portion of objects is under various types of severe occlusions caused by different factors.
  • Diverse motions. Humans have highly similar appearance, diverse motion and extreme articulation.

Challeng

In the visual world, objects rarely occur in isolation. The psychophysical and computational studies have demonstrated that human vision systems can perceive heavily occluded objects with contextual reasoning and association. The question then becomes, can our video understanding system perceive objects that are severely obscured? The OVIS competition will be hosted on an online platform and presentations will be delivered on Zoom.

Dataset
OVIS is a new large scale benchmark dataset for video instance segmentation task. It is designed with the philosophy of perceiving object occlusions in videos, which could reveal the complexity and the diversity of real-world scenes. OVIS consists of:
  • 296k high-quality instance masks
  • 25 commonly seen semantic categories
  • 901 videos with severe object occlusions
  • 5,223 unique instances

We use average precision (AP) at different intersection-over-union (IoU) thresholds and average recall (AR) as our evaluation metrics, following Youtube-VIS. The IoU in video instance segmentation is the sum of intersection area over the sum of union area across the video.
